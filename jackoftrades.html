Let us learn from the world’s richest man

“I believe you have to be willing to be misunderstood if you are going to innovate” - Jeff Bezos

India can share a new way of thinking.

Although India has had a history of being ruled by foreign intruders, most people in the region retained their faith and cultural identity. The entire country did not undergo conversion, either in the sphere of religion or culture. Why was it so?

A probable answer lies in the higher quality of Indian spiritual thought. Though physically subjugated, most people mind and hearts remained their own. Another reason could be the that religion was a very personal matter for most people of India, it was more about higher philosophy. They had an ista devta, personal god, and were steeped in ancient traditions; they could not bring themselves to surrender their souls to their oppressors. I say ‘higher philosophy’ because one cannot really call it religion as that would make it very narrow indeed -it is really a celebration of life, with deep insights.

Philosophy expounded in the Vedas, Patanjali yoga darshan, Upanishads, for example, transcends all caste, creed and physical boundaries and really ought to be the guiding principle for national and international discourses. This, together with high technology, should be the mantra of development for not only India, but the world. This would be the basis of a new philosophy.

Every act of violence for ethnic cleansing brings faith to the fore and the strong reaction to it forces the population to become even more fundamentalists. The whole basics of subjugation of one religion by another brings greater misery and more radicalisation.

Most religious authorities control the hearts and minds of the followers mostly by coercive means. In fact, all isma and religions have been misused for such control. The spirit of enquiry is missing.

Ancient India philosophical thought was based on the spirit of enquiry where the guru, the preceptor, only showed the way -the disciple had to find the goal. That is a higher form of thought process and is spiritual in nature rather than dogmatic.

The hearts and minds of people can be approached non-violently, by higher thought.no matter how criminal or evil person is, he can always be inspired by higher thought that is universal in nature.

Winning of hearts and minds through higher intellectual thought is the way forward in this time of ethnic strife and violence all over the world.

Similarly, Gandhiji, through non-violent ways, won the hearts and minds of the people. He distilled the ancient philosophy of Bhagwat gita and used it to raise the level of discourse to a higher level, thereby achieving remarkable results.

But for this transformation to happen, we need large numbers of evolved leaders like Adi Shankaracharya and MK Gandhi who understand the higher philosophical thought and who share their insights with the world. This, together with high technology, should be the development philosophy. This combination will enable us to live sustainably and efficiently and is likely to give us a sense of purpose and an emotionally satisfying life.

Nevertheless, for the world to follow this strategy, we have to first make it happen here in India in the past, all great thoughts from India went abroad after they were practised in India for a period of time. therefore, there is need for our own population to accept gracefully that there are different shades of opinion. If we understand the greatness of ancient Indian philosophical thought, that could help remove our fears and insecurities.

Let us learn from the world’s richest man

“I believe you have to be willing to be misunderstood if you are going to innovate”- Jeff Bezos, AMAZON

Luckily, we all want to grow in terms of beauty …luxury …so on.

But up to what sincerity we are working to achieve them?

Jeff Bezos-one of the worlds richest person and an inspiration for millions of entrepreneurs around the world. A journey that began with an online bookstore and cumulated into a global ecommerce giant has many lessons under its wrap which professionals from different fields are trying hard to emulate.

However, the best lessons we can learn from his failure not from the success of his brainchild amazon. Some of the biggest gaffes included the fire phone, register-credit card processing service for the certain bets which paid him rich dividends such as the kindle e-reader, and so he justified his choice and risk-taking approach as, “Bold bets pay for a lot of failures. I’ve made billions of dollars of failures at Amazon.com. we can’t be in survival mode. We have to be in growth mode.”

“if you are doing anything interesting in the world. you are going to have critics. You can’t stop it. move forward. it’s not worth losing any sleep over. When you receive criticism from the well-meaning people, it pays you to ask, ‘are they right? and if they are, you need to adapt what they’re doing. If they are not right, if you really have the conviction that they’re not right, you need to have that long-term willingness to be misunderstood. It’s a key part of innovation>” - Jeff Bezos

When you are going to do something-out of the box ,you are going have almost as many critics and haters as followers and admirers. Even the most successful people from any fields own them out in each step of their journey. But what sets them apart from commoners is their way of handling their critics and admirers at the same time. The bigger you aim the more ruckuses your critics would try to create to distract you. There is no possible way to escape criticism and the only way out it is by accepting them and taking the in your stride.

Humble origins and simplistic approach will win up a big smile for you. For a big in life focus on simple things and do them religiously and you would see the result sooner or later. There is always a win in discovering yourself – I…ME…MYSELF.
